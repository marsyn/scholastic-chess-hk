About Us

More Than a Chess Club —
A Complete Chess Education

Scholastic Chess Hong Kong is a chess education programme built around a professionally designed curriculum for children. Our club offers structured group lessons in small classes (max 4–6 students), guided by qualified coaches who follow a clear lesson format, a defined level system, and a research-backed teaching methodology created by our Head Coach, Dr. Pavel Martynov (PhD in Education, FIDE Master).

We don't just teach children how to move pieces. Our programme develops thinking skills through tactics, guided practice, and carefully selected classical games — progressing step by step from absolute beginner all the way to FIDE-rated tournament player.

Chess at our club is taught as an active game: children learn to play with initiative, creativity, and confidence.

Teaching Philosophy

How We Teach

At Scholastic Chess Hong Kong, we don't just teach moves — we build thinkers.

01

Effort Over Results

We celebrate hard work, curiosity, and persistence. Mistakes made in the pursuit of active play are treated as learning opportunities — never punished. Your child develops confidence and resilience in a safe, supportive environment.

02

Active, Attacking Chess

From the very beginning, we teach children to play with initiative and creativity. We encourage bold, active decisions rather than passive, fearful play — building a strong chess foundation and a sense of courage that extends beyond the board.

03

Structured Progression

Our curriculum follows four carefully designed levels — each with clear goals, specific knowledge requirements, and measurable outcomes. Your child always knows where they are and where they're headed.

04

Hobby to Professional Pathway

Whether your child plays for fun, cognitive development, or aspires to compete internationally — our programme supports every ambition. From the first lesson to FIDE-rated tournaments, we guide the entire journey.

Lesson Structure

Every Lesson — Structured,
Engaging, Purpose-Driven

Every group class follows a proven 60-minute lesson format designed by our Head Coach. Small groups of just 4–6 students ensure personalised attention for every child.

5–10 min

1. Warm-Up & Homework Review

Friendly check-in, homework review, reward stamps for effort and consistency.

5–10 min

2. ChessKid Review

Students share their online games and achievements from the week — building confidence and reflection.

15–30 min

3. Theory Block

One tactical idea + one model game + one brainy puzzle per lesson. Focused and structured learning.

5–10 min

4. Puzzle Time

Solve homework-related puzzles together, connecting theory to practice before independent work.

~20 min

5. Supervised Play

Students play real games while the coach provides live guidance and real-time feedback.

2–5 min

6. Homework & Rewards

Printed homework distributed; stickers and merit points awarded for effort and focus.

This is not "free play" — every minute is purposeful.
We call it Learning Through Play.

✎ Homework

Every student receives a printed homework sheet after each lesson, with puzzles directly connected to the topic covered in class. Homework is mandatory and reviewed at the start of each session. Sources include the Steps Method, Ivashchenko puzzle collections, and our Head Coach's proprietary databases.

Homework reinforces the lesson, builds independent thinking habits, and ensures steady progress between classes.
