## Slide 5 — CURRICULUM LEVELS

**Section label:** Curriculum

**Headline:**
> A Clear Path from First Move to Confident Player

**Intro body (2–3 lines):**
> Our school programme follows a structured, level-based curriculum. Each level has defined knowledge targets, clear promotion criteria, and an estimated timeframe — so both coaches and schools always know where students stand and where they're heading.

**Three levels (cards or a vertical pathway):**

---

**BEGINNER BOOTCAMP** *(Onboarding)*
*From absolute beginner to First Mover*

Students learn the history of chess, piece movement, rules of the game (check, checkmate, stalemate, castling, en passant), and basic notation. The Bootcamp uses age-appropriate methods designed for children as young as 4–6.

> Estimated time: 1 semester | ChessKid rating: 0–700

---

**FIRST MOVER** *(Level 1)*
*From knowing the basics to playing full, confident games*

Students master all chess rules, basic checkmates, the three golden rules of the opening, material values, and core tactical ideas. By the end of this level, students should be able to play independently in a tournament setting.

Core topics include: checkmate patterns, material advantage, phases of the game, classic miniature games, and an introduction to tactics and combinations.

> Estimated time: 2–4 semesters | ChessKid rating: 700–1100

---

**TACTICIAN** *(Level 2)*
*From casual player to strong tactical attacker*

Students dive into 25 key tactical patterns, attacking chess principles, and the foundations of positional play. This is where chess becomes truly engaging — students learn to launch attacks, punish mistakes, and develop their own playing style.

Core topics include: 25 tactical ideas, opening disasters, attacking principles I & II, introduction to positional play, aspects of chess strategy and planning.

> Estimated time: 4–8 semesters | ChessKid rating: 1000–1400

---

## Slide 6 — INSIDE A LESSON

**Section label:** Lesson Structure

**Headline:**
> Every Lesson. The Same Quality. Every Time.

**Intro body:**
> Our lessons follow a six-stage format designed by our Head Coach. It ensures every class is purposeful, engaging, and consistent — regardless of the coach or the school. Every minute has a role.

**The six stages (timeline or step layout):**

| # | Stage | Duration | What Happens |
|---|-------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Smooth Start** | 2–5 min | Attendance, brief check-in, lesson topic introduced. Sets expectations and classroom tone from the first minute. |
| 2 | **Chess Success Story** | 2–5 min | Students share highlights from their week on ChessKid — a game they're proud of, a puzzle they solved, a win or a lesson learned. Builds reflection and confidence. |
| 3 | **Theory Block** | 10–15 min | One tactical idea + one classical game analysed. Focused, structured, age-appropriate. The core teaching moment. |
| 4 | **ChessKid Mission** | 5–10 min | Coach reviews the weekly ChessKid task. Students are briefed on their independent work for the week — connecting in-class learning to home practice. |
| 5 | **Practice — Tournament** | 25–30 min | Students play tournament games against each other. The coach supervises and acts as arbiter. This is the heart of the lesson: theory becomes practice. |
| 6 | **Closing** | ~5 min | Lesson ends on a positive note. Merit Points acknowledged. School dismissal procedure followed. |

**Pull-quote / callout:**
> *"This is not free play — every minute is purposeful. We call it Learning Through Play."*

**Design notes:**
- Use a vertical timeline component (matching the lesson-step style from the parent site)
- Show the time allocation visually — a simple bar or timeline helps principals grasp the structure at a glance

## Slide 7 — HOW WE TEACH

**Section label:** Teaching Philosophy

**Headline:**
> We Don't Just Teach Chess. We Build Thinkers.

**Intro body:**
> At Scholastic Chess, a coach is not only a chess expert — they are a guide in the learning process. Our coaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that long-term improvement comes from structured learning, consistent effort, and a positive educational environment.

**Three pillars:**

**01 — Effort Over Results**
We celebrate hard work, curiosity, and persistence. Mistakes made in the pursuit of active play are treated as learning opportunities, never punished. Students develop confidence in a safe, supportive classroom environment.

**02 — Active, Attacking Chess**
From the very first lesson, we teach children to play with initiative and creativity. We encourage bold decisions rather than passive, fearful play — building both chess skills and a sense of courage that extends beyond the board.

**03 — The Coach Handbook**
Every Scholastic Chess coach works with our dedicated Coach Handbook — a comprehensive teaching supplement that covers classroom management strategies for large groups, advanced pedagogical techniques including blended learning and the station-rotation method, and guidance on the coach's role and best practices. Our coaches grow as educators, not just chess specialists.

## Slide 8 — DIGITAL TOOLS & ENGAGEMENT

**Section label:** Beyond the Classroom

**Headline:**
> Learning Doesn't Stop When the Bell Rings

**Body:**
> We extend chess learning beyond the classroom through a combination of digital tools, structured home practice, and a motivation system designed around effort rather than results.

**Three sections:**

**♟ ChessKid — Premium Subscriptions**
Every student receives a premium ChessKid account — the world's leading child-safe chess platform, powered by Chess.com. Students play games, solve puzzles, and watch chess lessons between sessions. Coaches monitor each student's activity in real time and use this data to personalise in-class instruction.

*ChessKid is used for Beginner Bootcamp through Tactician levels. Chess.com is introduced at Strategist level and above.*

**📋 Homework**
After each lesson, students take home a printed puzzle sheet directly tied to the topic taught in class. Homework is reviewed at the start of the following session. Sources include the Steps Method, Ivashchenko puzzle collections, and our Head Coach's proprietary database.

**⭐ Merit Points System**
Students earn Merit Points for weekly activities: attending lessons, solving puzzles, playing games, watching training videos, and completing homework. Top performers are recognised at the end of each term. The system promotes healthy study habits and intrinsic motivation — not just competitive ranking.

Merit points are awarded for: attending lessons · solving puzzles · playing games · watching chess videos · completing homework · tournament participation. Bonus points for consecutive weekly streaks.

## Content Notes for the Developer

### Slide order rationale
The flow is: Why Chess → Who We Are → What the Programme Is → How It Works (Levels → Lessons → Teaching → Digital) → Competitive Element → Team → Partnership → CTA. This mirrors the mental journey a principal takes: *Should I care? → Who are these people? → What exactly would happen? → Is it easy? → What's the next step?*

### Tone reminders
- Never use the word "sell" or imply urgency
- Avoid superlatives ("best", "leading") without evidence
- Frame everything from the school's/student's perspective, not Scholastic Chess's
- The curriculum document is a credibility asset — reference it but don't dump it on-screen
